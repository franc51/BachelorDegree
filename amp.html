Let's dive in. 

            In this particular case, the schematics has multiple stages which im going to explain.

            Firstly, we have a voltage amplification stage, composed by Q1 + Q2 + Q3, then the intermediate stage, 
            complementary transistors Q4, 5, 6, and 7 amplify the current and Q12 is biasing the final transistors.
            Lastly, transistors Q8, 9, 10 and 11 are power transistors which amplify the current more.
            Q13 and Q14 are current limiter transistors which limit the current passed through the final transistors.
            This is how a class AB stage is implemented.

            The most important aspect, in order to have a very stable and fully efficient amplifier, is to design 
            it in such a way that the biasing transistor sits on the same heatsink with the final transistors, in 
            order to ensure the thermal compensation.

            This amplifier is capable of outputting 100W on an 8 ohm load, having a distorsion factor of only 0.07%.
